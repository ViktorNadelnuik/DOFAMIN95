Vehicles must keep moving. Soldiers must come back alive.

Our platoon repairs trucks and armored vehicles right at the frontline so that the battalion can complete its missions, and vehicles can deliver ammo, troops and evacuate the wounded.

Your donation becomes a tool, a spare part, oil or welding material that gives a vehicle one more trip – and gives our people one more chance to return home alive.

About the platoon

We are the technical support platoon of a motorized infantry battalion – the unit that keeps the battalion’s vehicles operational on one of the most intense sectors of the frontline.

Our work is not always visible, but it directly affects whether a unit can complete its mission, whether a vehicle reaches its position, and whether the soldiers return alive.

What we do

• Maintenance
We perform scheduled and unscheduled maintenance, change oils, filters and fluids, and check the technical condition of vehicles. We make sure that each vehicle is ready to roll at any moment.

• Repair and restoration
We repair engines, gearboxes, suspension, braking systems, electrical systems, hydraulics and fuel systems. We handle everything – from small sensor replacements to major overhauls of large components.

• Vehicle recovery
When a vehicle can’t move under its own power, is damaged, stalled, or hit by enemy fire – we go out and evacuate it. Often we work literally under fire to pull vehicles off the “road of life”.

• Diagnostics and troubleshooting
We know our vehicles inside out, we “hear” faults, find hidden problems and fix them so that a vehicle doesn’t break down at the worst possible moment.

• Field repairs
Our workshop is whatever we can build ourselves: ground, tarpaulin, a flashlight, a generator and a set of tools. We work without proper lighting, in mud, under rain, at night, under shelling. No proper equipment? We work around it. No conditions? We create them.

• Keeping the battalion mobile
Operational vehicles mean ammo delivery, unit rotations, casualty evacuation, water and food supply, mobility and survival. We work to make sure these processes never stop.

Current needs

Your donations are currently turned into the following priorities:

• Tools and equipment for truck and armored vehicle repair
Socket sets, wrenches, extensions, jacks, pullers, suspension tools, pneumatic tools, extension cables and work lights.

• Welding equipment and consumables
Electrodes, welding wire, masks, gloves, extension cables and leads – everything needed to bring frames, bodies, mounts, fuel tanks and protective elements back to life.

• Spare parts and consumables
Filters, belts, brake pads, hoses, bearings, clamps, sensors, spark plugs and fixings. All the small things that stop big repair jobs if they are missing.

• Fluids and lubricants
Engine and transmission oil, brake fluid, coolant, greases for heavily loaded components.

• Diagnostics and electrics
Testers, multimeters, lamps, wires, terminals, connectors, fuses and contact groups – everything we need to quickly find and fix electrical problems.

• Logistics and basic working conditions
Generators, extension cables, LED lighting, site lights, plastic sheeting, tarpaulins, work gloves and workwear – so that we can keep working in field conditions in any weather.

Every euro, dollar or other contribution you send turns into a very specific thing in our hands: a tool, a spare part, oil or welding material – something that gives a vehicle one more run and our soldiers one more chance.
