About the platoon

We are the Technical Support Platoon of the mechanized infantry battalion of the 142nd Separate Mechanized Brigade. Our task is to keep the battalion’s vehicles running on one of the hottest directions of the front.

You rarely see us on camera or in the news, but our work determines whether a vehicle reaches the position, whether a unit receives ammunition and whether the wounded are evacuated in time.

What we do

• Maintenance
Planned and unscheduled service, oil, filters and fluid changes, technical condition checks. We do everything so that vehicles are ready to move at any moment.

• Repair and recovery
We repair engines, gearboxes, suspension, brakes, electrics, hydraulics and fuel systems. From small sensors to large assemblies.

• Vehicle recovery
When a vehicle cannot move on its own, is damaged, stuck or broken — we go for it. Often under fire, pulling vehicles off the “roads of life”.

• Diagnostics and fault finding
We know our vehicles “by sound”, catch hidden problems and fix them so they do not appear at a critical moment.

• Field repairs
Our workshop is what we can build from what we have: ground, tarp, flashlight, generator and tool set. We work without normal lighting, in mud, under rain, at night, under shelling. No conditions? Then we create them.

• Maintaining battalion mobility
Working vehicles mean ammo delivery, rotations, casualty evacuation, water, food, maneuver and survival. We do everything so that these processes do not stop.

Technical Support Platoon team

We work as a single mechanism. Everyone has their role, but the goal is the same — vehicles must move, and units must complete tasks and come back alive.

Platoon commander

Plans work, sets priorities, coordinates recovery and repairs. Makes decisions on what to save first so that units do not stop.

Deputy / senior master

Organizes work on site, monitors repair quality, keeps a log of tasks and responsible persons, maintains tempo and rotation.

Senior mechanic-driver / mechanic

Complex repairs, preparing vehicles for missions, defect detection of critical components, controlling safety and readiness.

Powertrain mechanic

Engine, clutch, gearbox/transfer case, axles, driveshafts, bearings. Replacement of assemblies and restoring functionality in the field and at base.

Suspension / chassis / brakes

Leaf springs, shock absorbers, hubs, brake system, steering. Eliminating play, leaks, overheating, vibration and jams.

Auto electrician

Wiring, starter/alternator, lights, fuses, grounds, relays, charging. Recovery after damage, searching for short circuits and hidden faults.

Diagnostics / scanner

Reading error codes, checking sensors/fuel/air intake, basic tests. Gives the exact cause of a fault so we do not lose time guessing.

Welder / body repair

Frame cracks, brackets, mounts, reinforcements, small body repairs. Quick restoration after impacts and vibration.

Tyres / wheels

Tyre repair, inflation, tube/valve replacement, balancing where possible, patching punctures. Preparing spares, controlling pressure under load.

Recovery team

Winching and towing, working with ropes/straps/winches, preparing routes and safe recovery. Extracting vehicles from risky areas.

Drivers (delivery / relocation)

Delivering spare parts and consumables, moving vehicles, delivering tools. Ensuring movement between the repair point and units.

Supply and logistics

Searching and delivering spare parts, oils, tools, consumables. Requests, inventory, resource allocation and stock control.

For security reasons, we deliberately do not show certain details. But behind every vehicle that returns to service there is a real team and hours of hard work.

Our mission

Our mission is to provide uninterrupted mobility for the battalion and save the lives of our soldiers.

We repair and restore trucks and armored vehicles directly in the combat zone, so that units can perform tasks without stopping.

We return to service the vehicles that transport ammunition, water, food, fuel and evacuate the wounded.

We work at night, under shelling, in rain and cold, without normal conditions — but we always finish the job.

Every tool, every part and every donation means one more vehicle trip, one more completed mission and one saved life.

Our principles

There are no coincidences in the work of a technical support platoon. We follow a few simple but strict principles that save vehicles and lives every day.

⚡ Speed

We react quickly to breakdowns, vehicle failures and unit requests. In the field every minute matters — we do not waste time.

🎯 Accuracy

Diagnostics, repair and adjustment — we aim for maximum quality. An error in repair can cost a vehicle or a soldier’s life.

🛠️ Professionalism

Everyone in the platoon knows their vehicles down to the last bolt. We put experience, knowledge and hands into every machine that must move.

💪 Responsibility

We understand the price of our work: a repaired vehicle = a completed mission. We are responsible for the result, not the process.

🚚 Mobility

We work anywhere: in the field, on dirt, under fire, in rain or frost. No workshop? We build one from what we have.

🔥 Resilience

We finish what we start. No excuses. Vehicles must move — and they will.

Why supporting the technical support platoon matters

The work of the Technical Support Platoon often stays “behind the scenes”, but without us no unit moves. Vehicles are the circulatory system of the front. If they stop — everything stops.

  • 🚚 Without trucks there is no delivery.
    Ammunition, water, food, rotations — everything depends on vehicles.
  • 🏥 Without working vehicles there is no casualty evacuation.
    If a vehicle does not start — the wounded may not reach stabilization in time.
  • ⚙️ Without repair there is no mobility.
    Infantry, UAV units, reconnaissance, engineers — all depend on the ability to move.
  • 🛠️ We repair directly at the frontline.
    We recover under fire, repair in the field, at night, in rain, by the light of a flashlight.
  • 🔥 Every donation = a concrete result.
    Tool → repair → vehicle back in service → mission completed → lives saved.

Supporting the Technical Support Platoon is one of the most effective contributions to defence: it directly turns into working vehicles and saved lives.

Questions and answers

Are you an official unit of the Armed Forces of Ukraine?

Yes. We are the Technical Support Platoon of the mechanized infantry battalion of the 142nd Separate Mechanized Brigade. All the vehicles we work with are officially assigned to AFU units.

What exactly do donations go to?

Primarily to tools, spare parts, consumables, oils, welding, diagnostics and basic conditions for a field workshop. In other words — directly to what allows us to repair trucks and armored vehicles so they can continue performing missions.

Can I help not with money but with tools or spare parts?

Yes. If you are ready to provide tools, equipment, spare parts or other materials — write to nadelnuikviktor@gmail.com or in Telegram @DOFAMIN95, and we will send a list of current needs and details regarding delivery.

Do you provide reports on donations?

Yes. For foundations, large donors and initiatives we can prepare a separate report: photos, list of purchased items, description of what the funds were used for and how it helped the platoon. Just contact us through the form on the site or directly via email.

Can we publicly mention your unit or use photos of vehicles?

Such things we coordinate separately, considering security. If you are a foundation, media or company and want to publicly mention the unit or use photos — write to us, and we will clarify what can and cannot be shown.
