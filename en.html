About the platoon

We are the Technical Support Platoon of the mechanized infantry battalion of the 142nd Brigade. Our job is to keep the battalion’s vehicles operational on one of the most demanding directions.

You will rarely see us in the spotlight, but our work decides whether a vehicle reaches positions, whether units receive ammunition and fuel, and whether wounded soldiers can be evacuated in time.

What we do

• Maintenance
Scheduled and unscheduled servicing: oils, filters, fluids, inspections — everything needed so vehicles are ready to move at any moment.

• Repairs and recovery
Engines, transmissions, suspension, brakes, electrics, hydraulics, fuel systems — from small sensors to major assemblies.

• Vehicle recovery
When a vehicle can’t move — broken, damaged, stuck — we go to retrieve it. Often under shelling and in difficult conditions.

• Diagnostics and troubleshooting
We locate hidden issues early and fix them before they become catastrophic.

• Field repairs
Our workshop can be a tarp, a lamp, a generator, and a tool kit. Mud, rain, night, frost — we work with what we have.

• Keeping the battalion mobile
Working vehicles mean ammo delivery, rotations, evacuation, water and food, maneuver and survival. We do what it takes to keep this running.

Technical Support Platoon team

We operate as one mechanism. Everyone has a role, but one goal — vehicles must move, and people must make it home alive.

Platoon commander

Plans work, sets priorities, coordinates recovery and repairs. Decides what to fix first so units don’t stop.

Deputy / senior mechanic

Organizes work on-site, controls quality, tracks tasks and responsibilities, maintains pace and continuity.

Senior driver-mechanic

Complex repairs, inspections, fault finding, safety checks, ensuring vehicles are ready for missions.

Powertrain / агрегate mechanic

Engine, clutch, gearbox/transfer case, axles, driveshafts, bearings. Replacements and recovery in field conditions.

Suspension / brakes

Springs, shocks, hubs, brake systems, steering. Fixing play, leaks, overheating, binding, vibrations.

Auto electrician

Wiring, starter/alternator, lighting, fuses, grounds, relays, charging. Restoring systems after damage and short circuits.

Diagnostics / scanner

Reading faults, checking sensors, fuel/inlet basics, quick tests. Saves time by finding the real cause fast.

Welder / structural work

Frame cracks, brackets, mounts, reinforcements. Fast recovery after impacts and heavy vibration.

Tires / wheels

Puncture repairs, inflating, valve/camera replacement, basic balancing. Preparing spares and keeping pressure under load.

Recovery crew

Towing and extraction, winches, straps, cables. Route prep and safe recovery from high-risk zones.

Drivers (delivery / reposition)

Delivering parts and consumables, moving vehicles between locations, ensuring mobility between repair points and units.

Supply & logistics

Finding and delivering parts, oils, tools, consumables. Requests, inventory, allocation, stock control.

Some details are intentionally not shown for security reasons. But every vehicle returned to service is the result of a real team and hours of hard work.

Current needs

Your donations currently turn into:

• Tools and equipment for trucks and armored vehicles
Socket sets, wrenches, extensions, jacks, pullers, suspension tools, pneumatic tools, extension cords, and reliable work lighting.

Our principles

There are no accidents in how we work. We follow a few simple — but strict — principles that help save vehicles and lives every day.

Speed

We respond quickly to breakdowns and unit requests. In the field, every minute matters.

Precision

Diagnostics and repairs are done properly. A mistake can cost a vehicle — or a life.

Professionalism

We know our vehicles down to the last bolt. Experience and skill go into every repair.

Accountability

A repaired vehicle means a mission can happen. We take responsibility for the result.

Mobility

We work anywhere — field, mud, rain, frost. If there is no workshop, we build one.

Resilience

We finish the job. No excuses. Vehicles must move — and they will.

FAQ

Are you an official unit of the Armed Forces of Ukraine?

Yes. We are the Technical Support Platoon of the mechanized infantry battalion of the 142nd Brigade. The vehicles we service are officially assigned to AFU units.

What exactly are donations used for?

Primarily: tools, spare parts, consumables, oils, welding supplies, diagnostics, and basic field-workshop conditions — everything that directly enables repairs.

Can I help with tools or parts instead of money?

Yes. If you can provide tools, equipment, parts, or materials — contact us at nadelnuikviktor@gmail.com or via Telegram @DOFAMIN95, and we will share the current list and delivery details.

Do you provide reporting on donations?

Yes. For foundations, companies, and major donors we can provide a separate report: photos, purchased items, and how the support improved our work.

Can we publicly mention your unit or use your photos?

We coordinate this case-by-case with security in mind. If you are a media outlet, foundation, or company — please contact us and we will clarify what can be shared.
